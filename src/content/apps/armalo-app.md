Armalo App is the flagship and the wedge. It is a multiplayer AI workspace —
several people and one durable agent in the same growing conversation — where a
team runs the full lifecycle of a business alongside the agent: planning,
designing, building, monitoring, and scaling. There is no demo video and no
waitlist: visiting [app.armalo.ai](https://app.armalo.ai) drops you into a
live workspace immediately.

That instant, shareable surface is the strategy. Consumer products spread by
being handed around; enterprise products get bought after they are already
inside the building. Armalo App is built to do both in order: an operator
tries it alone, pulls two teammates into the thread, the team outgrows the
free tier, and the company that now depends on it asks for the dedicated,
self-hosted version. The wedge runs from a free workspace to team seats to an
enterprise boundary — same product, same thread, deeper commitment at each
step.

### What the workspace does

- **Plan.** Strategic briefs, market maps, OKRs, hiring plans, and customer
  research assembled in the room; the same catalogue products the workspace
  produces are used by Armalo to plan this product.
- **Design.** Generative design briefs, side-by-side directions, and
  disposable tuning controls for websites, apps, and dashboards. See
  [Armalo Generative UI](/products/armalo-generative-ui) for the capability
  contract.
- **Build.** Vibe coding as the default. Hermes coordinates durable work;
  OpenCode handles heavy-code; Claude Code and Codex mount as task engines.
  The room event log is the build history.
- **Monitor.** Per-business dashboards (decision logs, run health, cost
  envelopes, risk) derived from the same event log. Group decisions become
  inspectable artefacts, not chat scrollback.
- **Scale.** Deep Stripe and Meta ads integrations, agentic browser
  automation, and a catalogue of operational agents the workspace composes.
  Spending and provisioning remain approval-gated; live cash-out and live ad
  spend require an approved idempotent proposal.

### Compass — the personalization mote

Compass is the personalization layer that lives inside every workspace. It
captures every decision the team makes or overrides as a structured
preference record, runs a confidence-gated autonomy dial over the resulting
graph, and lets the agent act silently when it is sure and ask when it is
not. The threshold rises as Compass fills in — the longer a team works in
the workspace, the fewer questions the agent has to ask on the same kind of
work.

That preference graph is the durable moat. The harness is copyable in a
weekend; six months of accumulated decisions, taste corrections, and
overrides is not. A team-compass layer sits above individual compasses so
shared workspaces resolve taste conflicts explicitly: team preferences outrank
role preferences, role preferences outrank member preferences, and the
disagreement itself becomes an observation Compass learns from. The data is
portable — the team owns their own observations and outcome history — so the
lock-in is honest: value creation, not hostage.

### Every autonomous action is bonded and reversible

Compensating Actions, Pacts, and bounded blast-radius are the safety
substrate that makes the autonomy claim credible. A high-risk action spends
through an approval port, leaves a typed compensating action, and can be
rolled back from the same event log that produced it. The user trusts the
agent spending their money overnight because the action is bonded before it
executes and reversible after — a sentence the autonomous-agent category
cannot say without owning the control plane.

### Operator quality compounds over time

The agent's quality improves as traces, outcomes, and skills feed back into
the prompt and policy layer. This is operator compounding, not a return
promise: every artefact the workspace produces makes the next one cheaper to
make. We surface the receipts — checks, evaluations, and decision logs —
because compounding only matters if it is inspectable.
