Expert Knowledge Assistant is a planned product for turning a coherent body of
work into a more accessible learning or advisory experience. It can be sold as
a standalone subscription, included with a course or community, licensed to a
team, or used as the front door to human advisory work.

The assistant must not pretend to be the expert, invent a private opinion, or
cross into regulated advice without the required controls. A real build needs
source rights, versioning, citations, correction workflows, identity language,
escalation, and evaluation against representative questions.
