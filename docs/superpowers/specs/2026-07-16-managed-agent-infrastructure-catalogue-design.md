# Managed Agent Infrastructure Catalogue Design

## Decision

Add the three approved pivots as distinct, planned products under one new
`Managed agent infrastructure` catalogue category:

1. **Managed Agent Workspaces** — the team-governance offer.
2. **Agent Skill Library** — the reusable domain-expertise offer.
3. **BYOK Agent Cloud** — the predictable managed-infrastructure offer.

The catalogue must not present raw VPS resale, a generic Hermes installation,
or an already-live service as the product. The value proposition is managed
coordination, reusable operating knowledge, and customer-controlled model spend.

## Public product contract

All three records use the existing content collection and public machine
projection. They are `planned`, `unavailable`, `not-yet-proven`, and at the
`build` flywheel stage. Each record names its audience, delivery and offer
shapes, intended operating boundary, and what is explicitly not yet available.

The records may describe SSO, centralized guardrails, skill packaging, backups,
gateway integrations, and bring-your-own-key operation as product direction.
They must not claim general availability, uptime, customer adoption, business
results, or production proof.

## Catalogue presentation

Place the family between agency infrastructure and AI engineering services in
the catalogue navigation. Reuse the existing restrained shelf and detail-page
design so the additions feel canonical rather than promotional. Each product
gets a full detail page automatically through the content collection.

## Durable rationale

Add a public operating thesis that explains why Armalo is not selling commodity
compute, how the three offers compose, who buys each one, the authority and cost
boundaries, and the evidence required before any proof status advances. Product
records link to that thesis.

## Verification

Add a dedicated source contract that parses all three records through
`appSchema`, verifies honest status/proof/access, checks their distinctive value
propositions, confirms catalogue grouping, and requires the public thesis. Add a
generated-output contract proving all three detail routes and both machine
catalogue surfaces contain the products after build. Run the complete portfolio
proof gate and visually inspect the homepage category plus all three routes.
