# Consumer Wedge to Enterprise: Why app.armalo.ai Leads the Portfolio

Status: adopted 2026-07-15. Owner: Armalo AI. This document frames how the
portfolio presents itself and how the product ladder is sequenced. It is a
strategy statement, not a revenue claim — proof discipline on the catalogue
(status, access, proof, flywheel stage) still governs every public claim.

## The one-line thesis

**Armalo App (app.armalo.ai) is our likeliest viral consumer product, and the
consumer motion is how we break into enterprise.** Everything else in the
catalogue is packaged output of that machine.

## Why consumer first

Enterprise software is bought slowly, by committee, on references we do not
yet have. Consumer software is _tried_ — instantly, alone, with no
permission. The classic wedge (Slack, Notion, Figma, Vercel) is not a
metaphor; it is a repeatable mechanism:

1. **An individual adopts** because trying costs nothing and the first minute
   is delightful.
2. **The product is inherently shared**, so adoption pulls in teammates
   without a sales touch.
3. **The team's work accumulates inside the product**, which converts
   curiosity into dependence.
4. **The company inherits the dependency** and buys the enterprise wrapper:
   security, tenancy, control, support.

Armalo App is built so each step is structural, not aspirational:

- **Step 1 is the URL.** Visiting app.armalo.ai mints a live workspace with
  the agent already present. No sign-up wall, no demo video, no waitlist. The
  first minute _is_ the product.
- **Step 2 is the thread.** The unit of work is a multiplayer conversation —
  several people, one AI, one growing thread on a shared canvas. Sharing a
  workspace is how you use it, so distribution is a side effect of usage.
- **Step 3 is the accumulated workspace.** The software a team builds, the
  conversation that built it, and the running product live in the same place.
  Leaving means abandoning the thing you made and the context that made it.
- **Step 4 is the published ladder.** Starter (free, bring-your-own keys) →
  Team (paid seats plus usage) → Enterprise & Agency (dedicated, self-host /
  on-prem, white-label). The enterprise tier is the same product inside the
  customer's boundary — not a second codebase.

## Why this particular product can go viral

Most AI coding tools are single-player with sharing bolted on. Armalo App's
differentiators are the consumer mechanics themselves:

- **Zero-friction entry.** The distance from "heard about it" to "inside a
  live workspace" is one click. Every removed step compounds through a viral
  funnel.
- **Multiplayer by default.** Presence, shared canvas, and a common thread
  with the agent make the product legible to spectators — watching someone
  build is itself the pitch.
- **A free tier with honest economics.** Bring-your-own-keys makes the free
  tier sustainable rather than a loss-leader with a fuse.
- **Output you can hand someone.** Sessions end in working software on a real
  URL — an artifact that markets the tool that made it.

## How the wedge feeds enterprise

The enterprise offer is not a pivot; it is the top rung of the same ladder.
The path we sell:

1. **Land** — a builder or small team uses the public app for real work.
2. **Expand** — paid Team seats, more workspaces, production hosting and
   custom domains.
3. **Harden** — the buyer's requirements shift from features to boundary:
   SSO/SCIM, audit export, data residency, dedicated cluster, self-host or
   air-gap, white-label.
4. **Operate** — the done-for-you lane: Armalo builds and runs entire
   processes for the customer inside their deployment.

By the time step 3 begins, the champion, the proof, and the workload already
exist inside the account. That is the entire point of leading with consumer:
enterprise deals start at step 3 instead of step 0.

## What this means for the rest of the catalogue

The catalogue's product families (customer service desks, revenue agents,
CRM, knowledge assistants, and the rest) are **outputs of the flagship, not
siblings of it**:

- Armalo builds and operates catalogue products inside Armalo App. The
  catalogue is the flagship's proof of work.
- Each catalogue sale is also an enterprise on-ramp: a delivered product is a
  running instance of the machine, inside a customer's business.
- The portfolio therefore reads in this order: the consumer flagship first,
  then the shelf of packaged offers it produces.

## Honesty constraints on this framing

The site's proof rules apply to the thesis, not just the entries:

- Armalo App is presented as **live / public / publicly verified**, at the
  **launch** flywheel stage: the public surface and paid plans are live, and
  it is earning its first users. "Viral" names the design and the bet, not a
  measured growth curve.
- Monetize and compound stage claims require verified revenue, and we will
  not advance the flagship's stage ahead of that evidence.
- Enterprise capabilities listed on the pricing surface (self-host,
  white-label, SSO/SCIM) are the offer being sold; deployments are scoped
  per-customer.

## The metric that matters

The wedge succeeds in this order, and we track it in this order:

1. First-session activation: visitor → live workspace → first agent turn.
2. Multiplayer spread: workspaces with ≥2 humans.
3. Conversion: free workspace → first paid dollar.
4. Boundary ask: first inbound request for the dedicated/self-host tier.

Until (3) is repeatedly true, everything else on this site stays framed as
inventory and hypotheses, per the catalogue's proof discipline.
